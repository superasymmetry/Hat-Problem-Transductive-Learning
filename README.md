# Hat-Problem-Transductive-Learning

Have you ever heard of the famous "hats" puzzle game? It is when three people (players, prisoners, humans abducted by aliens, whatever you imagine...) each wear a red or blue colored hat on their heads. They cannot see their own hat, but can see everyone else's hat and thus knows their hat colors. The players collectively win if at least one of them guesses their own hat color correctly, with no one guessing incorrectly. This is actually quite similar to transductive learning in statistical learning theory.
